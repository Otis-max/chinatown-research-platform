About Project

Project Overview

Chinatown is a place where overseas Chinese have come together in solidarity to build their lives. Over a century ago, Chinese immigrants crossed oceans to seek opportunities around the world, bringing with them the rich traditions and culture of China. Chinatown stands as a vivid testament to that historical journey. As international Chinese students, we deeply admire the rich culture created by earlier generations of Chinese immigrants through their perseverance in foreign land, so we launched the project Landscaping and Memories: A Digital Humanities Mapping of Chinatown. It aims to explore, document, and share the rich cultural landscapes of Chinatowns around the world through digital humanities methods, while fostering a deeper understanding and recognition of cultural identity.

You will find interactive timelines, maps, and data visualizations showcasing the current global distribution of Chinatowns, the development history of representative Chinatowns, nearly two centuries of Chinese migration waves and trends, the ten most populous Chinatowns worldwide, and a historical timeline of the Chinese Exclusion Acts.
